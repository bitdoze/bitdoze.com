## FAQs

- ### What is the Razer Thunderbolt™ 5 Dock?




It’s a powerful Thunderbolt 5 docking station that expands your laptop or desktop connectivity with 11 ports, including an M.2 SSD slot, support for triple 4K displays at 144 Hz, and fast data transfer speeds up to 120 Gb/s (80 Gb/s for data and 40 Gb/s for Video).

[Learn More](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/learn/thunderbolt-5-for-gaming.html?wapkw=thunderbolt "Thunderbolt™ 5 for Gaming") about Thunderbolt™ 5 for Gaming

- ### Which laptops are compatible with the Thunderbolt 5 Dock?




The dock works with any laptop or desktop featuring a Thunderbolt™ 4, 5, or USB-C port that supports Thunderbolt protocols.

- ### How many monitors can I connect to the dock?




You can connect up to three 4K monitors running at 144 Hz or an 8K display at 60 Hz for an ultra-high-resolution multi-monitor setup.

- ### Does the dock support laptop charging?




Yes, it delivers up to 140 W power delivery with active cooling, enabling fast and stable passthrough charging for your laptop without needing a separate AC adapter.

**Display Restrictions Notes**

1\. The HDMI 2.1 port and DisplayPort 2.1 port cannot be used simultaneously.

2\. The front USB-C ports support charging and data transfer but do not support video output.3. Windows laptops with USB-C ports that support DisplayPort Alt Mode can only connect to one external monitor. Apple Silicon MacBooks with M1 / M2 / M3 Standard chips support only one external display via Thunderbolt. MacBooks with M1 / M2 / M3 Pro or Max chips, and all M4 models, support dual displays. M4 MacBooks with Thunderbolt 5 do not support three displays over a single Thunderbolt 5 connection.4. This dock supports triple display via two Thunderbolt downstream ports and one HDMI or DP port. However, whether triple display can be achieved depends on your laptop's specifications. Very few laptops are capable of supporting triple display with this dock. Based on our testing, only the Razer Blade 18 Gaming Laptop with NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090 successfully supports triple display. We will continue to update the list as more compatible devices become available.5. For MacBook (M Pro-series), connect the standard monitor before the high refresh rate monitor. Otherwise, the second display may not be recognized, especially after sleep.6. This dock does not support dual 5K Thunderbolt 3 monitors or a combination of a 5K Thunderbolt 3 monitor and a Thunderbolt 4 monitor in screen mirroring mode. For example, LG UltraFine 5K and Apple Studio Display are not supported in such configurations.

**System Dependencies Notes**

1\. The minimum macOS version required to use this Thunderbolt 5 dock is macOS 15 or later.

2\. MacBooks with Apple Silicon chips do not support external GPUs through any docking station. This is a system-level limitation that prevents macOS from recognizing or using an external GPU, even if it is physically connected via a dock.

3\. If your laptop uses an 11th Gen Intel processor, please ensure that "Thunderbolt Technology Support" is enabled in the BIOS to maintain optimal performance. Also, confirm that the Thunderbolt controller NVM firmware version is 48.1 or higher.

## 140W Power Delivery

The TS5 Plus provides up to 140W power delivery for even the most demanding laptops. This makes it ideal for laptops like the 16″ MacBook Pro with M3/M4 Pro or Max chips that require 140W.

Whilst many other Thunderbolt 5 docks state that they can charge at up to a certain wattage, they actually include an insufficient power supply. These docks will allocate host power based on how many devices are connected to them. Some of the host power will move to newly attached devices, thereby reducing the power delivery.

Rest assured that the CalDigit TS5 Plus’ power supply will always provide the full 140W power delivery, and will never drop that performance even when new devices are connected to the TS5 Plus.

For laptops or tablets that require less than 140W, the TS5 Plus will only supply the wattage required by your device.

## Dual USB Controllers

The TS5 Plus is a unique device, and this is ultimately demonstrated by being the only dock with dual USB controllers\*.

Traditional Thunderbolt docks feature a single USB controller that all of the USB, and Thunderbolt ports on the dock share. The TS5 Plus’ dual controllers allow the dock to provide two individual 10Gb/s controllers for increased bandwidth to connected devices. This is a great solution for anyone adding multiple high bandwidth USB devices to their dock.

_\*The second USB controller is only supported on Thunderbolt based hosts, as well as USB4 hosts that support PCIe. Please contact your USB4 host manufacturer for PCIe support information._

What is the minimum macOS specification required to use the TS5 Plus?

macOS 15 and above.

What is the minimum Windows OS specification required to use the TS5 Plus?

Windows 11 and above.

Does the TS5 Plus work with USB-C host devices?

No. The TS5 is only compatible with Thunderbolt 5, Thunderbolt 4, Thunderbolt 3, USB 4 v2, or USB4 host devices. It is not compatible with 5Gb/s or 10Gb/s USB-C host devices.

Does the TS5 Plus work on a Thunderbolt 4 or Thunderbolt 3 computer?

Yes, the TS5 Plus is compatible with Thunderbolt 4 Macs and Windows PCs. However, the TS5 Plus is only compatible with Thunderbolt 3 Macs. It is not compatible with Thunderbolt 3 Windows PCs. Users on Thunderbolt 4 or Thunderbolt 3 host computers will see reduced functionality due to the fact that those versions of Thunderbolt provide half the performance of Thunderbolt 5.

Does the TS5 Plus work on a Mac featuring the M1-M4, M1-M4 Pro, M1-M4 Max, M1-M4 Max, and M1-M3 Ultra?

Yes, the TS5 Plus is the perfect companion for the above Apple Silicon Macs.

Base M1-M3 Macs only support a single display through the Thunderbolt connection.

Pro, Max, M4 base, and Ultra Mac users can connect dual displays to the TS5 Plus.

Three displays are not supported through a dock or hub on M4 Thunderbolt 5 Macs via a single Thunderbolt 5 connection.

Does the TS5 Plus work with a USB4 or USB4 v2 computer?

Yes, the TS5 Plus works with computers that are USB4 or USB4 v2, and can support PCIe over USB4.

What is Bandwidth Boost?

A new feature of Thunderbolt 5 is called Bandwidth Boost, and it allows the TS5 Plus to allocate more performance when you connect bandwidth intensive displays to the dock, such as high-refresh rate displays, or multiple high-resolution displays. It will allocate up to 120Gb/s to the displays, and leave 40Gb/s for the rest of the dock.

How do I connect a single monitor to the TS5 Plus?

**Option 1 (DisplayPort Monitor)**

Directly connect a DisplayPort monitor to the DisplayPort connector.

Or

Connect a USB-C to DisplayPort Video Adapter (sold separately) to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports.

**Option 2 (Thunderbolt Monitor)**

Directly connect a Thunderbolt monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports on the TS5 Plus.

**Option 3 (USB-C Monitor)**

Directly connect a USB-C monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports on the TS5 Plus.

**Option 4 (HDMI Monitor)**

Add an Active DisplayPort to HDMI Adapter (sold separately) to the DisplayPort connector.

Or

Connect a USB-C to HDMI Video Adapter (sold separately) to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports.

How do I connect dual monitors to the TS5 Plus?

**Option 1**

– Connect one monitor to the DisplayPort connector.

– Connect the second monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports via USB-C Video Adapter (sold separately).

**Option 2**

– Connect one monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports via a USB-C Video Adapter (sold separately).

– Connect the second monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports via a USB-C Video Adapter (sold separately).

**Option 3**

– Connect two USB-C or Thunderbolt monitors directly to two of the Thunderbolt 5 ports.

How do I connect three monitors to the TS5 Plus (Windows Thunderbolt 5 Only)?

– Connect one monitor to the DisplayPort connector.

– Connect the second monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports via a USB-C Video Adapter (sold separately), or directly connect a USB-C or Thunderbolt monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports.

– Connect the third monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports via USB-C Video Adapter (sold separately), or directly connect a USB-C or Thunderbolt monitor to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports.

Can I connect a non-DisplayPort monitor to the DisplayPort connector?

Yes. For example, if you want to connect an HDMI monitor, you can connect an Active DisplayPort to HDMI Adapter (sold separately).

Make sure to use an “Active” adapter.

Is the TS5 Plus fully compatible with the Apple Pro Display XDR at full resolution?

Yes, you can directly connect the Apple Pro Display XDR to one of the spare Thunderbolt 5 ports and output at full 6K resolution.

What is the maximum laptop charging capability of the TS5 Plus?

140W.

If the Host Thunderbolt 5 port charges at 140W what do the Downstream Thunderbolt 5 ports charge at?

The two downstream Thunderbolt 5 ports charge at 36W each.

If my laptop charges at less than 140W does the TS5 Plus scale down the charging?

Yes, if your laptop charges at less than 140W, the TS5 Plus will only supply the amount of power your laptop requires.

If I am using a computer that doesn’t require power from a dock, such as the M4 Mac mini, does the TS5 Plus still supply power?

If your are using a Mac mini, iMac, or desktop computer the TS5 Plus will not supply power via the Thunderbolt 5 connection.

Can I use all 10 USB ports on the TS5 Plus at the same time?

Yes, you can use all the ports on the TS5 Plus at the same time.

Why does the front USB-C port charge at 36W?

The front 36W USB-C port is ideal for use with USB-C devices that require more power, such as the iPhone 16 Pro Max that charges at up to 36W.

Can I use both the SD and microSD card readers at the same time?

Yes, both card readers can be use at the same time.

Can I use both audio outputs at the same time?

No. When two audio outputs are connected, the system will default to the front audio output. You can only use one audio output at a time.

What cables can I use with the TS5 Plus?

For the optimal compatibility we recommend to use certified 80Gb/s Thunderbolt 4 cables with 240W power delivery.
