# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Developers and DevOps engineers. They arrive from search with a concrete problem — a Docker container to fix, a VPS to configure, a tool to self-host — mid-task and short on patience. Their job: get a working setup fast, with configs and commands they can copy-paste and trust. The site publishes in English and Spanish, so guides must read cleanly for a bilingual technical readership.

## Product Purpose

Bitdoze publishes practical DevOps, programming, and self-hosting guides that have been tested end to end. It exists to grow organic search traffic and convert it into affiliate revenue by recommending the tools and products the guides genuinely use. Success is a first-time searcher landing on a guide, solving their problem, and trusting the site's recommendations enough to act on them.

## Positioning

Guides that actually work. Every article is tested, copy-paste-ready, and current — no fluff, no outdated steps, no theory without a terminal.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: click the affiliate recommendations inside articles. Fallback: browse related guides and stay on-site.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "DevOps guides that actually work."
- Belief ladder: (1) this guide actually solved my problem — the article delivers value first; (2) the author tests what they recommend — trust transfers from the guide to the picks; (3) this product is worth paying for — the affiliate click feels safe.
- Proof on hand: 10+ years of IT experience with 4+ years focused on DevOps; an archive of 400+ published guides (335 English, 64 Spanish); the @webdoze YouTube channel and social audience; real client work from the services business.

## Brand Personality

Practical, direct, trustworthy. The voice is a working practitioner who respects the reader's time: says what works, shows the config, skips the preamble. Reference point for the feel: DigitalOcean's tutorial pages — clean, scannable, code-forward, calm.

## Anti-references

- Ad-cluttered tutorial farms — content buried under ads, popups, and affiliate spam; the SEO content-farm feel.
- The generic AI-blog aesthetic — purple gradients, glassmorphism, glow effects, interchangeable SaaS-style pages.
- Corporate docs portals — dry, impersonal enterprise documentation look and tone.
- Medium/Substack clones — an indistinguishable generic reading platform with no identity of its own.

## Design Principles

1. The guide is the proof. Every page earns trust by solving the reader's problem before it asks for anything; recommendations come after delivered value, never before.
2. Tested, not transcribed. Real configs, real commands, real versions. If a step wasn't run, it doesn't ship.
3. Recommend like a practitioner. Product picks read as advice from someone who runs them — embedded in the workflow, clearly reasoned, never a billboard.
4. Respect the reader's time. Scannable structure, the answer findable in seconds, zero filler between the reader and the terminal block they came for.
5. One reader, one next step. Each page offers a single clear primary action and a quiet fallback into related guides — never a wall of competing CTAs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text at 4.5:1 contrast or better in both light and dark themes, visible focus states, reduced-motion support. Code blocks stay legible and navigable with keyboard and screen readers, since they're the site's core artifact.
